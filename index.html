Inflation Rate at British Columbia

CPI is a common indicator of inflation rate. It is the ratio of (current price/price of the base period).

As you can see, it seldom goes down over the last 6 years. Recently the index almost skyrockets -- not a good sign.

12-month % change

A more informative indicator is the change of CPI compared to last year.

The problem is much more clear -- The growth rate increases dramatically in 2022!

OK. So what about the funding...
The cost of living in vancouver is really scary. But a poor funding can make things worse...
Oh no!

From our survey, the funding of 47.5% graduate students at UBC CS department cannot offset basic expenses!

Here, the funding means the sum of TA, RA, and awards.

With support...

Some lucky ones get extra financial support, and some have internship as additional source of income. All students that receive extra support can afford basic expenses. Good for them!

However, we notice that students that are in urgent need for financial support currently have no extra support available.

Also, even those with "always enough" funding, they are still very close to the boundary (y=x), which means that it is impossible to have saving.

We're feeling unsafe!

88.6% of the respondents are feeling unsafe in current grad stipend amount. This is so concerning -- Even students that can afford basic expenses with funding still feel unsafe!
